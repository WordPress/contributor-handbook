# Design: Contribute to Photos

**Type:** Project (Bounded work, may take multiple weeks)

Upload at least 30 high-quality, CC0-licensed photos of your campus, town, or meaningful surroundings to the WordPress Photo Directory. Your photos support contributors worldwide by providing free images for WordPress sites. Your work becomes part of the WordPress project.

**Setup**

* **Connect:** Join [\#photos](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/C0337A01LHW) Slack channel and say hello  
* **Camera:**   
  * You can take photos using any camera: smartphone, mirrorless/DSLR, or tablet. Modern mobile cameras work perfectly well as long as the image quality meets the guidelines.  
* **Skills & References:**   
  * Photo Guidelines: [https://wordpress.org/photos/guidelines/](https://wordpress.org/photos/guidelines/)	 

## **Steps**

**Start:** Where to find the work & choose what to pick first, then:

1. Choose a theme such as campus life, architecture or nature. Plan to submit at least 30 usable images.   
2. Start taking photos that are:    
- Sharp, well-lit, and in focus  
- Free of logos, trademarks, or recognizable faces  
- Lightly edited and your original work  
3. Save your photos as:   
- JPEG format  
- At least 2000 × 2000 pixels  
- Between 1–20 MB in file size  
4. Review your photos. Make sure each photo meets the [WordPress Photo Guidelines](https://wordpress.org/photos/guidelines/).  
5. Submit your photos here: [https://wordpress.org/photos/submit/](https://wordpress.org/photos/submit/)  
- Add alternative text describing what can be seen in the image for accessibility  
- Confirm the license and guideline checklist  
- Submit 1 photo at a time. You can have up to 5 photos in the moderation queue at once.  
- After submitting, you will see this message on the submission page:

*“Submissions awaiting moderation*  
*You can have up to 5 photos in the moderation queue at a time. You currently have \[number of previously submitted photos\].”*

6. Wait for the email confirming whether your submissions were approved or not. Once reviewed, you can submit more photos.  
   

## **Finish**

**How to know it’s done well:**

- [ ] You uploaded at least 30 photos

- [ ] You received an email confirming whether your submissions were approved

- [ ] Moderators approved your 30 photos

- [ ] Your WordPress.org profile shows your photo contributions (Your [WordPress.org](http://WordPress.org) profile \> click Photos on the left side) 

- [ ] Your photos appear publicly in the WordPress Photo Directory [https://wordpress.org/photos/author/YOUR-USERNAME/](https://wordpress.org/photos/author/YOUR-USERNAME/)  
- [ ] Your [WordPress.org](http://WordPress.org) profile displays the Photo Contributor badge (purple circle with a camera icon).

**Followup:**

* After moderators approve your photos, share your contribution in  [\#photos](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/C0337A01LHW) on Slack and ask for feedback.

## **FAQs**

**Q: I’m stuck, where should I go for help?**

A: First, review the [Photo Guidelines](%20https://wordpress.org/photos/guidelines/) or [Photos FAQ](https://wordpress.org/photos/faq/) page. If you still have questions, post in [\#photos](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/C0337A01LHW) on Slack and share your question.

**Q: How many photos can I submit at once?**

A: You can submit 1 photo at a time and have up to 5 photos awaiting moderation. You will receive an email once moderators review your submissions.

**Q: What should I do if a photo is declined?**

A: You will receive an email at the address linked to your WordPress.org account from **photos@wordpress.org** explaining why your photo was declined. Read the reason carefully. Update your photo if possible, then submit it again when ready.  
   
**Q: What should I do if I see an error while uploading a photo?** 

A:  If your photo meets the [WordPress Photo Guidelines](https://wordpress.org/photos/guidelines/) and you still see an error, report the issue here:  
[https://meta.trac.wordpress.org/newticket?component=Photo%20Directory](https://meta.trac.wordpress.org/newticket?component=Photo%20Directory)   
Describe what happened and include any relevant details, such as the error message, your browser, or a screenshot.
