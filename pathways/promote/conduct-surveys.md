# Conduct Surveys

**Function:** Promote  
**Type:** Task  
**Level:** Beginner  

Gather insights about how people use WordPress and identify areas where awareness is low. Design a survey, collect responses, and share your findings in #community-team and with your local group. Fresh perspectives, especially from students and newer community members, help the project understand what's working and what needs attention.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** Choose a survey tool you're comfortable with. Free options like [Google Forms](https://docs.google.com/forms/) or [Jotform](https://www.jotform.com/) work well.
- **Read:** Review the [2021-2022 Meetup Survey Key Findings](https://make.wordpress.org/community/2023/05/02/2021-2022-wordpress-meetup-survey-key-findings/) to see what a community survey looks like in practice, and look at the [WordPress Event Attendee Feedback form](https://form.jotform.com/WordPress_Community/wordpress-event-attendee-feedback) as an example of how the Community team gathers feedback.
- **Connect:** Join [#community-team](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/community-team) on Slack and introduce yourself.

## Steps

1. **Decide what you want to learn.** Pick a topic related to how people use WordPress, what they struggle with, or where awareness is low. A focused question produces more useful results. The [Community Team Handbook](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/) is a good place to explore what the community cares about.
2. **Design your survey.** Keep it short and easy to complete. Use multiple choice and rating scales where possible — they're easier to analyze than open text fields. If you're new to writing survey questions, these guides are helpful starting points: [Jotform Survey Guide](https://www.jotform.com/how-to-create-a-survey/), [SurveyMonkey Survey Design Guide](https://www.surveymonkey.com/mp/survey-guidelines/), [Pew Research on Writing Survey Questions](https://www.pewresearch.org/our-methods/u-s-surveys/writing-survey-questions/), and [Harvard's Questionnaire Design Tips](https://psr.iq.harvard.edu/book/questionnaire-design-tip-sheet).
3. **Share the survey.** Distribute it through whatever channels make sense — your local meetup, student club, online WordPress spaces, or your own network. If you need to reach people by email, tools like [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/help/getting-started-with-mailchimp/) or [WordPress newsletter plugins](https://wordpress.org/plugins/tags/newsletter/) can help. Give people enough time to respond.
4. **Summarize your findings.** Look for patterns and surprises. Write up what you asked, who responded, and what you learned.
5. **Share what you found.** Post your summary in [#community-team](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/community-team) on Slack and share it with your local group. You can also share in [#community-events](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/community-events) if your survey relates to WordPress events.

## Contribution checklist

- Survey designed with a clear focus
- Distributed with enough time to collect responses
- Findings summarized and shared in #community-team and with your local group

## What happens next

Your findings may inform how the community plans events, resources, or outreach. If your results spark interest, the team may ask you to present at a meeting or write it up for the [Make WordPress Community blog](https://make.wordpress.org/community/).
