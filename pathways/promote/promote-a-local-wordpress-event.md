# Promote a Local WordPress Event

**Function:** Promote  
**Type:** Project  
**Level:** Beginner  

Help a local WordPress event reach more people by running a short promotional campaign. Create flyers, share on social media, and spread the word in your community. This is a great fit for contributors with marketing or communication skills who want to support their local WordPress community and reach people who aren't online.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** No special tools required. A basic design tool like [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) or a word processor is enough for creating flyers. For social media, use whatever platforms you're active on
- **Read:** Read through the [Publicity](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/wordcamp-organizer/planning-details/publicity/) page in the WordCamp Organizer Handbook for tried and tested promotion ideas
- **Connect:** Join [#community-events](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/community-events) on Slack and introduce yourself

## Steps

1. **Find a local in-person event.** Browse [upcoming WordPress events](https://events.wordpress.org/upcoming-events/filtered/format/in-person/) and find one near you. Meetups, WordCamps, and other WordPress gatherings all benefit from extra promotion.
2. **Reach out to the organizers.** Click through to the event listing and contact the organizers via the event website or meetup.com. Let them know you'd like to help promote the event and ask what they need most.
3. **Plan your campaign.** Work with the organizers to decide what would be most useful. This might include creating flyers and posters, sharing on social media, reaching out to local tech groups, or contacting universities and co-working spaces. The [Tuesday Training promotion guide](https://make.wordpress.org/community/2021/09/28/tuesdays-training-how-to-promote-wordpress-meetup/) has practical ideas for both online and offline outreach.
4. **Create and distribute your materials.** For offline promotion, place flyers in strategic locations such as coffee shops with Wi-Fi, co-working spaces, libraries, and college notice boards. The [Banners and Flyers](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/wordcamp-organizer/planning-details/banners-and-flyers/) page has downloadable templates you can adapt. For online promotion, share the event across your social media, in local Facebook groups, on LinkedIn, and with any relevant communities you belong to.
5. **Share what you did.** After the event, post a brief summary in [#community-events](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/community-events) on Slack about what you promoted, which channels you used, and what worked. This helps other contributors learn from your experience.

## Contribution checklist

- Connected with the event organizers and agreed on what's needed
- Promotional materials created and distributed online, offline, or both
- Summary of your campaign shared in #community-events

## What happens next

The organizers will see the impact of your work through attendance and engagement. If the event hasn't happened yet, keep promoting until the day arrives. After the event, check in with the organizers to hear how it went.

From here, you can promote another event or get more involved with the Community team by helping organize events yourself.
