# Design: Create Block Patterns

**Function:** Design  
**Type:** Task

Block patterns are ready-made layouts you can insert into a page or post, such as a newsletter signup or a pricing section. In this guide, you'll learn how to find and use patterns from the Block Pattern Directory, then create and submit your own pattern.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/contribution-pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** Use [WordPress Playground](https://wordpress.org/playground/) to test your pattern before you submit it.
- **Read:** [Submitting a pattern to the directory](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/submit-your-block-pattern-to-the-directory/) — this is the main guide you'll follow.
- **Reference:** New to the Block editor or patterns? These will help:
  - Lesson: [Introduction to Block(site) editor](https://learn.wordpress.org/lesson/intro-to-the-site-editor/)
  - Documentation: [Block Editor](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/wordpress-block-editor/)
  - Documentation: [Block Pattern](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/block-pattern/)
  - Documentation: [Block Pattern Directory](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/block-pattern-directory/)
  - Video tutorials: [Using Block Patterns](https://wordpress.tv/2023/09/18/using-block-patterns-4/)
  - Video tutorials: [Intro to Block patterns](https://learn.wordpress.org/tutorial/intro-to-block-patterns/)

## Steps

1. Go to [**Add a new pattern**](https://wordpress.org/patterns/new-pattern/) on WordPress.org to start your submission.
2. Follow the steps in [Submit your block pattern](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/submit-your-block-pattern-to-the-directory/#how-to-submit-a-block-pattern) to the directory.
3. Before you submit, add a clear **title** and **description** so people know what your pattern is and when to use it. For example:
   - **Title:** Bold sale banner with geometric background
   - **Description:** A banner with a sales message and a call-to-action button
4. Choose a **category**. Categories help people find patterns and determine where they appear in the editor.

## Contribution checklist

- [ ] Go to [**My Patterns**](https://wordpress.org/patterns/my-patterns/) to view your patterns (draft, submitted, published, and pending review).
- [ ] Verify you can edit drafts and patterns that are still under review.
- [ ] When your pattern is approved and published in the public directory, you'll receive an email.

## What happens next

When your pattern is approved and published in the public directory, you'll receive an email.

When you're ready, submit another pattern, explore a new category, or browse the directory for inspiration.
