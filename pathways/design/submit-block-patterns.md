# Design: Create Block Patterns

**Function:** Design  
**Type:** Project  
**Level:** Beginner

Block patterns are ready-made layouts you can insert into a page or post, such as a newsletter signup or a pricing section. In this guide, you'll learn what makes a good pattern, then design and submit patterns to the Block Pattern Directory.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/contribution-pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** Use [WordPress Playground](https://wordpress.org/playground/) to test your patterns before you submit them
- **Read:** [A quick guide for building WordPress patterns](https://wordpress.org/patterns/about/) and [Submit your block pattern to the directory](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/submit-your-block-pattern-to-the-directory/)
- **Connect:** Join [#themes](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/C02RP4Y3K) and introduce yourself

## Steps

1. Browse the [Block Pattern Directory](https://wordpress.org/patterns/) to see what patterns exist and get ideas for what you might create.
2. Design and build your first pattern in the WordPress Block editor.
3. Test your pattern in [WordPress Playground](https://wordpress.org/playground/) to make sure it looks and works as expected.
4. Go to [Add a new pattern](https://wordpress.org/patterns/new-pattern/) on WordPress.org.
5. Add a clear **title** and **description** so people know what your pattern is and when to use it. For example:
   - **Title:** Bold sale banner with geometric background
   - **Description:** A banner with a sales message and a call-to-action button
6. Choose a **category** to help people find your pattern and determine where it appears in the editor.
7. Follow the steps in [Submit your block pattern to the directory](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/submit-your-block-pattern-to-the-directory/#how-to-submit-a-block-pattern) to submit for review.

## Contribution checklist

- You have copyright or legal rights to submit your patterns
- Each pattern has a descriptive title and description
- Each pattern combines multiple blocks and isn't a full page
- Your patterns work with different themes

## What happens next

Your patterns will go to review. When each is approved and published, you'll receive an email.

When you're ready, design and submit more patterns, explore new categories, or consider auditing other patterns in the [Block Pattern Directory](https://wordpress.org/patterns/).
