# Join the Patterns Moderation Team

You'll review community-submitted block patterns and approve or decline them based on quality, uniqueness, and usefulness. Moderators keep the Block Pattern Directory a reliable resource for contributors worldwide.

- **Prerequisite:** [Submit Block Patterns](https://make.wordpress.org/handbook/pathways/design/submit-block-patterns/) with at least 10 published patterns, and be involved in the #patterns channel
- **Reference:** [Submit your block pattern to the directory](https://wordpress.org/documentation/article/submit-your-block-pattern-to-the-directory/) — review requirements
- **Connect:** Post in [#patterns](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/patterns) that you'd like to help review patterns

## Steps

1. **Request to join the moderation team.** Post in #patterns expressing your interest. Once approved, you'll get access to the moderation queue.

2. **Access the moderation queue.** Log in to [WordPress.org](https://wordpress.org) and navigate to the pattern moderation queue.

3. **Review a pattern.** Select a pattern and verify it meets the guidelines: unique, useful, works across themes, and uses appropriate content.

4. **Test the pattern.** Open it in [WordPress Playground](https://wordpress.org/playground/) with different themes to verify it works as expected.

5. **Approve or decline.** If you decline, select a reason so the contributor knows what to improve.

6. **Work in small batches.** When there are multiple patterns to review, review one pattern at a time rather than rushing through.

## Contribution checklist

- Successfully joined the moderation team
- Completed your first batch of pattern reviews

## What happens next

Your moderation decisions take effect immediately. Continue to moderate regularly and build familiarity with edge cases.

## Help

Stuck? Check the [getting help guide](https://make.wordpress.org/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/#getting-help), then ask in [#patterns](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/patterns). Search the channel first to see if someone has already answered your question.
