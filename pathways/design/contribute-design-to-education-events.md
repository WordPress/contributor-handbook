# Contribute Design to Education Events

**Function:** Design  
**Type:** Project  
**Level:** Beginner

Design badges, graphics, and event materials for WordPress education programs like Campus Connect and WP Credits. Your work directly supports programs that bring new contributors into the WordPress project.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/contribution-pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** Create a free [Figma](https://www.figma.com/) account.
- **Read:** [WordPress Education Programs project board](https://github.com/orgs/WordPress/projects/277/views/1) — browse open design requests before you start.
- **Connect:** Join [#campusconnect](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/campusconnect) and [#wpcredits](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/wpcredits) on Slack and introduce yourself

## Steps

1. Go to the [WordPress Education Programs project board](https://github.com/orgs/WordPress/projects/277/views/1) and filter by the Design label. If no requests are available, check back later or ask in [#campusconnect](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/campusconnect) or [#wpcredits](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/wpcredits).
2. Choose a request from the Ready column and leave a comment to introduce yourself and let others know you're working on it. If the request is already In Progress, leave a comment and wait for acknowledgment before starting.
3. Share the issue link in [#campusconnect](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/campusconnect) or [#wpcredits](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/wpcredits) so organizers are aware — GitHub notifications can be missed.
4. Start designing. Clarify requirements (size, format, deadline) in the issue comments, and share early drafts for feedback before finalising.
5. When your design is complete, comment on the issue with your final file, a Figma link if applicable, and exported assets in the requested format (usually SVG unless otherwise specified).
6. Wait for confirmation from the requester. They may approve the design, request revisions, or mark the issue as Done. Once approved and marked Done, your contribution is complete.

## Contribution checklist

- Design approved by the requester
- Issue marked Done
- Final file uploaded or linked in the issue
- Completed work shared in [#campusconnect](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/campusconnect) or [#wpcredits](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/wpcredits)

## What happens next

The requester reviews your submission directly in the GitHub issue. This can take a few days. If you haven't heard back after 48 hours, leave a follow-up comment on the issue tagging the requester. If you still don't hear back, post in [#campusconnect](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/campusconnect) or [#wpcredits](https://wordpress.slack.com/messages/wpcredits).

Once your contribution is complete, you can pick up another request from the board or explore other [Design pathways](/handbook/contribution-pathways/).
