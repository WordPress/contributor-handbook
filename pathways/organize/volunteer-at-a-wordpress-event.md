# Organize: Volunteer at a WordPress Event

**Function**: Organize  
**Type**: Task  
**Level**: Beginner

Help make a WordPress event welcoming, organized, and accessible for everyone who attends. Volunteering at a WordCamp is a great first contribution because you can support the community in practical ways, meet other contributors, and learn how WordPress events work from the inside.

## Before you start

**Setup:** Complete the [common setup](https://make.wordpress.org/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first

**Read:**
- [Welcome – Make WordPress Community](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/community-team/)
- [Volunteers – Make WordPress Community](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/wordcamp-organizer/planning-details/volunteers/)
- [Code of Conduct – Make WordPress Community](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/wordcamp-organizer/planning-details/code-of-conduct/)

**Connect:** Join [`#community-team`](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/C037W5S7X) on Slack and introduce yourself.

## Steps

1. **Find an event that needs volunteers**. Visit [events.wordpress.org](https://events.wordpress.org/) and look for a local WordCamp or other official WordPress event. Check the event site for a page like “Volunteer,” “Call for Volunteers,” or “Get Involved.”

2. **Read the event details**. Review the event schedule, venue details, Code of Conduct, and volunteer information. Pay attention to the time commitment, any deadlines, and the roles the organizers are recruiting for.

3. **Apply to volunteer**. Fill out the volunteer form if one is available. Share your availability clearly, note any relevant experience, and mention any preferences or accessibility needs. If you are new to WordPress events, that is okay, WordCamps welcome first-time volunteers.

4. **Join the event communication channels**. Watch for follow-up from the volunteer team by email or Slack. Once accepted, join any event-specific communication channels and make sure you know how organizers will reach you before and during the event.

5. **Learn your role**. Read any volunteer briefing or schedule you receive. Common volunteer roles include registration desk, information desk, swag station, room support, accessibility support, greeter or roamer, speaker support, runner, and Contributor Day support.

6. **Attend orientation if offered**. Some events offer a volunteer briefing, online onboarding, or venue tour. Attend if you can. This is the best way to learn the layout, understand expectations, and know who to contact when questions come up.

7. **Show up ready to help**. Arrive early for your shift, wear any volunteer badge or shirt provided, and be friendly and proactive. Help attendees with directions, questions, check-in, and general event flow. If you do not know the answer to something, ask an organizer instead of guessing.

8. **Support a welcoming and inclusive event**. Follow the [Code of Conduct](https://make.wordpress.org/community/handbook/wordcamp-organizer/planning-details/code-of-conduct/) and help others do the same. Be respectful, collaborative, and mindful of attendees who may need extra support, including people with accessibility needs. If you notice a safety, conduct, or accessibility issue, escalate it to an organizer right away.

9. **Help contributors get started, if assigned**. At Contributor Day, volunteers may help newcomers find the right table, create a WordPress.org account, join Slack, or learn about contributor teams. A welcoming first conversation can make a big difference for someone new.

10. **Wrap up and stay connected**. Finish your shift responsibilities, return any supplies, and thank the organizers. If the event collects feedback, share what you learned. If you enjoyed the experience, volunteer again or explore organizing and other community contributions.

## Contribution checklist

- [ ] Found an official WordPress event to volunteer at
- [ ] Read the volunteer information and Code of Conduct
- [ ] Applied or contacted organizers about volunteering
- [ ] Once accepted, joined the required communication channel
- [ ] Reviewed your assigned role and schedule
- [ ] Attended orientation or read the volunteer briefing
- [ ] Completed at least one volunteer shift
- [ ] Helped attendees, speakers, or contributors during the event
- [ ] Escalated questions or issues appropriately
- [ ] Shared feedback or stayed connected after the event
