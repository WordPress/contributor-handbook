# Triage Theme Check Issues and PRs

The Theme Check plugin validates themes against WordPress directory requirements, and every theme reviewer depends on it. Help keep it healthy by triaging open issues and pull requests: confirm bugs, test fixes, categorize enhancement requests, and flag stale items.

- **Prerequisite:** Comfortable with WordPress themes, familiar with how Theme Check works, and able to read PHP code well enough to evaluate whether an issue is valid
- **Reference:** [Themes triage handbook](https://make.wordpress.org/themes/handbook/get-involved/themes-triage/) — the team's triage workflow, priorities, and how champions are assigned
- **Connect:** Join [#themes](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/themes) on Slack and introduce yourself

## Steps

1. **Set up your testing environment.** Open [WordPress Playground](https://playground.wordpress.net/) and install the [Theme Check](https://wordpress.org/plugins/theme-check/) plugin.

2. **Browse the open issues.** Go to the [Theme Check issues](https://github.com/WordPress/theme-check/issues) on GitHub and read through what's been reported and what's outstanding.

3. **Confirm or reproduce bugs.** Pick an issue that reports a bug. Follow the steps described, try to reproduce the problem, and comment with your findings — including your environment, what you tested, and whether you could reproduce it.

4. **Test open pull requests.** Check the [open PRs](https://github.com/WordPress/theme-check/pulls) and test whether the proposed changes work as intended and don't introduce new problems. Comment with your test results.

5. **Categorize and flag.** Note which issues are bugs, enhancement requests, duplicates, or no longer relevant. Flag stale items or anything that needs a decision in #themes or during a [team meeting](https://make.wordpress.org/meetings/#themes).

## Contribution checklist

- Reviewed the open issues and PRs in the Theme Check repo
- Confirmed or tested at least one issue or PR with documented findings
- Commented on the issue or PR with your results

## What happens next

A Themes team member or maintainer may respond to your comments, adjust labels, or assign a champion to the issue. Don't worry if your assessment gets corrected — that's part of learning the triage process.

As you get comfortable, attend team meetings to participate in triage discussions. Consistent triage work is one of the most valuable contributions you can make to keep the plugin moving forward.
