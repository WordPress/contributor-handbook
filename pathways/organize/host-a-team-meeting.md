# Host a Team Meeting

**Function:** Organize  
**Type:** Task  
**Level:** Intermediate

Help keep your Make WordPress team running by hosting a meeting or taking notes. Most teams need volunteers for these roles every week, and it's one of the most direct ways to contribute once you've settled into a team.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Complete:** You should have already joined a Make WordPress team, attended several of their meetings, and be familiar with how they run
- **Read:** Take the [Community Meeting Etiquette](https://learn.wordpress.org/course/community-meeting-etiquette/) course on Learn WordPress
- **Connect:** Join the Slack channel for your Make team. The [Make WordPress teams page](https://make.wordpress.org/) lists each team's channel and their next scheduled meeting time

## Steps

1. **Attend several meetings as a participant.** Get a feel for the format, the agenda structure, and how the facilitator and note-taker work together. Read a few published meeting notes on your team's blog to understand what the output looks like.
2. **Volunteer to take notes.** At the end of most meetings, the facilitator asks for a note-taker and a facilitator for the next meeting. Note-taking is a good first step because you're capturing what's discussed and publishing it on the team blog. Your team's handbook will have guidance on the format.
3. **Volunteer to facilitate.** Once you're comfortable with the flow, offer to run a meeting. The facilitator writes the agenda, announces each item, keeps the discussion moving, and asks for volunteers for the next meeting. You don't need to be an expert on every topic. You're guiding the conversation, not leading it.
4. **Publish the agenda or notes.** Depending on your role, publish the agenda before the meeting or the notes after it on your team's Make blog. If you don't have blog access, ask a team rep to help.

## Contribution checklist

- Attended several meetings and read published meeting notes for your team
- Volunteered to take notes or facilitate
- Agenda or meeting notes published on the team blog

## What happens next

Most teams need facilitators and note-takers every week, so there's always an opportunity to do it again. As you facilitate more meetings, you'll build a deeper understanding of your team's projects and priorities. Some teams rotate the role regularly; others rely on whoever volunteers.
