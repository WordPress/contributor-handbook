# Review a Pathway Guide

Help keep pathways accurate and newcomer-friendly by walking through a guide as if it's your first time. Check that it covers everything a new contributor needs — note where you get stuck, flag broken links, check for drift from team handbooks, and file an issue with what you find.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Setup:** Familiarize yourself with the [pathways directory](https://github.com/WordPress/contributor-handbook/tree/feature/pathways/pathways) and the [project board](https://github.com/orgs/WordPress/projects/282).
- **Read:** Review [TEMPLATE.md](https://github.com/WordPress/contributor-handbook/blob/feature/pathways/pathways/TEMPLATE.md) to understand how pathways are structured, and the [About Pathways](/handbook/pathways/about/) section to understand their purpose. Pathways should point to team handbooks, not duplicate them.
- **Connect:** Join the Slack channel for the team the pathway relates to ([find your team's channel here](https://make.wordpress.org/)) — you may need to check with them if something seems out of date.

## Steps

1. **Pick a pathway to review.** Choose a published pathway or a PR that needs review from the project board. If reviewing a PR, leave a comment so others know you're on it.
2. **Walk the pathway as a newcomer.** Follow every step from the top as if it's your first contribution. Don't skip anything — actually click the links, install the tools, and try the process. Note every point where you get stuck, confused, or have to guess what to do next.
3. **Check all links.** Verify that every link works, points to the right place, and hasn't moved or been replaced.
4. **Check for drift.** Compare the pathway's instructions against the current team handbook(s) it references. If the pathway says one thing and the handbook says another, flag it. If the handbook is outdated, note that too — handbooks should be updated alongside pathways.
5. **Check structure and tone.** Does the pathway follow [TEMPLATE.md](https://github.com/WordPress/contributor-handbook/blob/feature/pathways/pathways/TEMPLATE.md)? Is it concise and actionable? Does it link out to handbooks rather than duplicating them? Does it follow the [WordPress Brand Writing Style Guide](https://make.wordpress.org/marketing/handbook/resources/style-guide-and-brand-book/)?
6. **File an issue or leave a PR review.** Report what you found — what worked, what didn't, broken links, drift from handbooks, missing steps, and anything confusing. Be specific and constructive.

## Contribution checklist

- [ ] Walked the entire pathway end-to-end as a newcomer
- [ ] Verified all links work and point to the correct resources
- [ ] Compared instructions against current team handbook(s) and flagged any drift
- [ ] Confirmed the pathway links to handbooks rather than duplicating them
- [ ] Checked structure against TEMPLATE.md and tone against the Writing Style Guide
- [ ] Filed an issue or left a PR review with specific, constructive findings

## What happens next

The pathway author or a maintainer will review your feedback. If you flagged handbook drift, that may result in updates to the handbook as well as the pathway. If there's no response after a week, a polite ping in the relevant team's Slack is fine.

When you're ready, review another pathway or try [creating one](/handbook/contribution-pathways/create-a-pathway-guide/).

## Help

Stuck? Check the [getting help guide](/handbook/contribution-pathways/before-you-begin/#getting-help), then ask in the relevant team's Slack channel.
