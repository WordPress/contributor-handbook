# Contribute to Data Liberation

**Function:** Build  
**Type:** Team  
**Level:** Intermediate  

Join the Data Liberation team and help build tools for importing, exporting, and migrating WordPress data across platforms. The project's goal is to make content truly portable across the web, with no lock-in or barriers. This is ongoing development work that spans multiple phases and requires experience with PHP and WordPress development.

## Before you start

Complete the [common setup](/handbook/pathways/before-you-begin/) first, then:

- **Complete:** You should be comfortable with PHP, familiar with WordPress core development, and able to work with GitHub repositories and pull requests
- **Read:** Read through the [Data Liberation project overview](https://wordpress.org/data-liberation/) to understand the vision and the five development phases. Then explore the [phase discussions on GitHub](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions) to see where active work is happening
- **Connect:** Join [#data-liberation](https://wordpress.slack.com/archives/data-liberation) on Slack and introduce yourself. Let the team know what skills you bring and where you'd like to help

## Steps

1. **Understand the five phases.** Each phase tackles a different part of the data portability problem. Read through the discussions for each to find where your skills fit best:
   - [Phase 1: Migration Guides and Community Tools](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions/77)
   - [Phase 2: Importing and Exporting Structured Data](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions/78)
   - [Phase 3: Liberating Data From Closed Platforms](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions/79)
   - [Phase 4: Direct WordPress-to-WordPress Synchronization](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions/80)
   - [Phase 5: Content Creation Powerhouse](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions/81)
2. **Explore the repositories.** Development happens across the [Data Liberation repo](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation) and the [WordPress Playground repo](https://github.com/WordPress/wordpress-playground) where much of the importer work lives. Browse open issues and PRs to get a sense of what's being worked on and where help is needed.
3. **Pick an issue to work on.** Look for [open issues](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/issues) in the Data Liberation repo, including any tagged [good first issue](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/labels/good%20first%20issue). If nothing matches your skills, ask in #data-liberation what the team needs most right now.
4. **Submit your first pull request.** Fork the repo, work on the issue, and open a PR. Reference the issue in your PR description. Let the team know in #data-liberation that your PR is ready for review.
5. **Stay involved.** Follow the [GitHub discussions](https://github.com/WordPress/data-liberation/discussions) and participate in #data-liberation on Slack. As you contribute more, you'll build context on the project and be able to take on larger pieces of work.

## Contribution checklist

- Explored the five phases and identified where your skills fit
- Claimed an issue or joined a discussion in the repo
- First PR submitted and shared in #data-liberation
- Following GitHub discussions and Slack to stay connected with the team

## What happens next

A maintainer will review your PR and may request changes. If there's no response after two weeks, follow up in #data-liberation. As you contribute consistently, you'll gain deeper context on the project's direction and be able to take on more complex work across the phases.
